INTERNAL RAG EVALUATION NOTES
Team: AI Platform Engineering
Last Updated: 2024-Q4

=== EVALUATION METHODOLOGY ===

We ran a 3-week evaluation sprint on our internal RAG system covering 200 hand-annotated query-answer pairs. Key findings below.

Faithfulness scores:
- With llama3.2 as judge: avg 0.73 across our test set
- With mistral as judge: avg 0.68 (lower consistency observed)
- Human agreement with llama3.2 judge: 81% on binary faithful/not-faithful classification

We recommend llama3.2 at temperature=0 as our internal judge model going forward. The consistency advantage over larger models is offset by the lower cost and speed.

=== RETRIEVAL QUALITY OBSERVATIONS ===

Top-k sweep (k=1,3,5,10):
- k=3: best precision, acceptable recall for our query distribution
- k=5: recall improves by 12%, but faithfulness drops 8% (more noisy context)
- k=10: diminishing returns, latency increases unacceptably

Conclusion: k=3 is our default, k=5 for multi-hop queries.

Chunk size sweep (128, 256, 512, 1024 tokens):
- 512 tokens with 50-token overlap performed best on our internal NDCG benchmark
- 256 tokens had higher precision but poor recall on long-answer queries
- 1024 tokens degraded faithfulness by 15% (too much irrelevant context)

=== MEMORY SYSTEM EXPERIMENTS ===

We implemented a query memory system that caches successful retrieval plans. Results after 2 weeks of dog-fooding:

Memory hit rate: 34% of queries matched a cached plan (>60% word overlap)
Average retrieval improvement on memory hits: +0.09 relevance score vs fresh plans
Memory false positive rate: 8% (cached plan used for semantically different query)

Recommendation: Memory threshold at 0.75 avg relevance for save; 60% word overlap for match. Cap at 50 entries with LRU eviction.

=== AGENTIC VS NAIVE RAG COMPARISON ===

We compared our agentic multi-source RAG against a simple single-source (vector DB only) baseline on 50 multi-source queries:

Agentic RAG:
- Source coverage: 94% (got info from all expected sources)
- Overall answer quality: 0.81 avg score
- Avg latency: 4.2s

Naive RAG (vector DB only):
- Source coverage: 61% (missed filesystem and API sources entirely)
- Overall answer quality: 0.64 avg score
- Avg latency: 1.1s

The quality improvement of agentic RAG justifies the latency cost for our use case (internal knowledge assistant).

=== KNOWN ISSUES ===

1. Planner occasionally selects "api" source for historical queries that should only use "vector_db". Adding few-shot examples to planner prompt reduced this by 40%.

2. Evaluator convergence: 7% of queries hit max_iterations (3) without meeting confidence threshold. These tend to be highly ambiguous queries.

3. Memory pollution: Without the 0.75 relevance threshold, old low-quality plans got cached and degraded subsequent queries. The threshold is critical.
